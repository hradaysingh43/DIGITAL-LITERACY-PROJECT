Reflection

While researching this topic, what surprised me the most was how common and easily executed online frauds have become, especially through UPI and digital payment platforms. I had always assumed that cybercrimes required advanced technical skills, but I discovered that many scams rely on simple tricks like fake calls, phishing links, or misleading payment requests. It was shocking to learn that even educated individuals and college students often fall victim to these scams due to lack of awareness or acting in a hurry. The fact that a single click or entering a UPI PIN can lead to instant financial loss was particularly alarming.

Another surprising aspect was how quickly scammers create a sense of urgency or trust, pretending to be bank officials, delivery agents, or even friends. This psychological manipulation makes it difficult for people to recognize fraud in the moment.

As a result of this research, one important habit I will change is becoming more cautious and mindful while using digital platforms. I will always double-check the authenticity of messages, links, and payment requests before taking any action. I will also avoid sharing personal or financial information with anyone and will educate my friends and family about safe online practices. Adopting these habits will help me stay secure and make responsible use of digital technology.
