
A real problem caused by poor digital communication occurred during a group project in an online class. One team member misunderstood a message about the deadline because it was written unclearly in the group chat. The message said “submit by Friday,” but didn’t specify the time or time zone. Some members assumed it meant midnight, while others thought it meant during class hours. As a result, part of the project was submitted late, which affected the group’s overall grade. This situation shows how vague wording in digital communication can lead to confusion and mistakes. Clear, specific messages—such as including exact times and confirming understanding—are important to avoid such problems, especially when people rely entirely on online communication.
